Top 10 Buffalo Bills

1. Jim Kelly
Pro Football Hall of Fame, 5× Pro Bowl, 4× AFC Champion
The legendary quarterback who led the Bills to four consecutive Super Bowls in the early 1990s. Kelly's leadership, toughness, and command of the K-Gun offense made him the face of the franchise and a Buffalo icon.
2. Bruce Smith
Pro Football Hall of Fame, NFL All-Time Sack Leader, 11× Pro Bowl
The NFL's all-time sack leader, Smith was a dominant force on the defensive line. His relentless pass rush and run-stopping ability made him one of the greatest defensive players in league history.
3. Thurman Thomas
Pro Football Hall of Fame, NFL MVP (1991), 5× Pro Bowl
A versatile running back, Thomas was the engine of the Bills' offense during their Super Bowl years. He could run, catch, and block, making him a matchup nightmare for defenses.
4. Andre Reed
Pro Football Hall of Fame, 7× Pro Bowl, 4× AFC Champion
One of the most reliable receivers of his era, Reed was known for his toughness and ability to make big plays in crucial moments. He remains the franchise's all-time leader in receptions and receiving yards.
5. O.J. Simpson
Pro Football Hall of Fame, NFL MVP (1973), 6× Pro Bowl
The first player to rush for over 2,000 yards in a season, Simpson was a transcendent talent in the 1970s. His speed and vision made him one of the most electrifying runners in NFL history.
6. Stefon Diggs
3× Pro Bowl, NFL Receptions Leader (2020)
A modern superstar, Diggs transformed the Bills' passing attack after arriving in 2020. His route running, hands, and playmaking ability have made him Josh Allen's favorite target and a fan favorite.
7. Josh Allen
2× Pro Bowl, 2020 AFC East Champion, Franchise QB
The current face of the franchise, Allen's arm strength, athleticism, and leadership have brought the Bills back to prominence. He continues to set records and inspire hope for a Super Bowl title.
8. Cornelius Bennett
5× Pro Bowl, 3× First-team All-Pro, 4× AFC Champion
A cornerstone of the Bills' dominant defense in the late 1980s and early 1990s, Bennett was a versatile linebacker who could rush the passer, stop the run, and cover receivers.
9. Fred Jackson
Bills Wall of Fame, 5,646 rushing yards, 30 rushing TDs
An undrafted fan favorite, Jackson's hard-nosed running style and perseverance made him a beloved figure in Buffalo. He consistently produced for the team during some of its leanest years.
10. Steve Tasker
7× Pro Bowl, NFL 1990s All-Decade Team, Special Teams Ace
Arguably the greatest special teams player in NFL history, Tasker's impact went far beyond the stat sheet. His tackling, blocking, and leadership were vital to the Bills' success.
